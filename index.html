About

A short introduction

My M.Eng. thesis is MyFOC — a from-scratch Field-Oriented Control library in C/C++ for BLDC/PMSM motors, with three-phase current sensing and an impedance mode that turns a motor into a programmable spring–damper for arm rehabilitation. Before grad school, my B.Eng. capstone was a 140 W HF linear amplifier (1.8–30 MHz) for emergency communications.

In industry, I built a Python test-automation framework at Avigilon (Motorola Solutions) and worked on hydrogen fuel-cell systems at Loop Energy. I'm most at home with EV powertrains, batteries, motor control, and embedded firmware — but I'm interested in anything where firmware meets hardware meets the real world.
